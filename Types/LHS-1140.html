About LHS-1140

LHS 1140 b is a rocky exoplanet located approximately 40 light-years away from Earth, orbiting the red dwarf star LHS 1140 in the constellation Cetus.

It was discovered in 2017 and is considered one of the most promising candidates for the search for life beyond our solar system due to its size, composition, and location in the star's habitable zone.

Physical Characteristics

  • Mass: About 6.48 times that of Earth.
  • Size: Roughly 1.6 times Earth's radius, making it a super-Earth.
  • Orbital Period: 24.7 Earth days.
  • Distance from Star: 0.09 AU (inside the habitable zone, where liquid water could potentially exist).
  • Star Type: LHS 1140 is a red dwarf, smaller and cooler than the Sun.

Conditions on LHS-1140

LHS 1140 b is thought to have a rocky surface similar to Earth. Due to its larger mass and density, it likely has a strong gravitational pull. Although the exact atmospheric conditions remain unknown, its location in the habitable zone suggests that it could potentially support liquid water if an atmosphere is present.
